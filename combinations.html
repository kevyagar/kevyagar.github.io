Question: You are hanging out with your friends. There's only two people who you want to hangout with at a time, but you have 4 friends, Alice, Bob, Charlie, and Daniel. How many ways can you pick 2 friends to hangout with among 4 friends?

Solution: You can hangout with Bob and Charlie, which is the same as Charlie and Bob. In this case, you can list out every possible combination of friends and remove the duplicate, or you can use the formula.

The formula for calculating the number of combinations for r items chosen from a total of n items is denoted C(n, r). So the number of combinations for choosing two friends to go out with from four friends is C(4, 2). The formula for calculating C(n, r) is:
